Candidate for Nairobi Branch Organizing Secretary – KUPPET

I am Dominic Mutuku Mutua, a passionate and tech-driven educationist with deep roots in the teaching profession and an unwavering commitment to educational reform and teachers’ welfare. My leadership approach is grounded in service, integrity, and innovation, championing the transformation of union operations through digital integration and inclusive policies.

Vision

To create a more transparent, digitally-empowered, and teacher-first union culture that uplifts every educator’s dignity, welfare, and career trajectory.

My Manifesto – 20 Points for a Better Union

Enhanced Member Communication

Develop a secure members' database for direct SMS/email communications, moving beyond unreliable WhatsApp groups.

Direct Allowance Payment via MPESA

Champion for instant allowance payments via MPESA based on automated attendance tracking.

Blended AGM Participation

Ensure AGMs are accessible online (Zoom/Google Meet) for broader participation and cost-effectiveness.

Social & Networking Events

Establish wellness events like team-building retreats and sports tournaments for teacher cohesion and mental wellness.

Proper Budgeting & Legal Support

Initiate a legal defense fund to protect teachers when deductions are irregular or dues are withheld.

Harmonious Leadership

Maintain respectful collaboration with elected officials to prioritize teachers’ needs over internal politics.

Empowering Teachers

Deploy tech tools (apps, bulk SMS) to collect teachers’ opinions before union decisions are finalized.

Teachers’ Participation in Constitution

Enable members to give input on union constitutional changes through surveys and digital platforms.

Honesty & Selflessness

Run a transparent and teacher-first office grounded in honesty and ethical leadership.

Leverage Digital Technologies

Support e-voting, digital registration, and online grievance systems to modernize KUPPET operations.

Better Medical Cover

Challenge inefficient Minet/SHA health contracts and advocate for access to quality healthcare.

End Unfair Teaching Beyond 9th Lesson

Defend teachers from exploitation by enforcing strict adherence to official teaching limits.

Champion for JSS Teachers

Advocate for urban hardship allowances, medical cover, and quick confirmation for Nairobi-based JSS interns.

Independent JSS Leadership

Demand JSSs be managed independently from primary schools, resisting corruption in headship appointments.

Merit-Based Promotions

Ensure fair and transparent promotion processes across gender and qualifications.

Digital Voting

Introduce online voting for teacher convenience, transparency, and lower election costs.

Teachers’ Slots in Housing Projects

Secure fair housing allocations for teachers in national housing schemes.

Independent JSS Sports

Advocate for JSS to manage their own sports programs—autonomous from primary school leadership.

Awareness & Prevention Forums

Initiate forums to address and prevent misconduct and false accusations.

Policy Review & Legal Reform

Advocate for research-driven review of legal definitions and fair hearing for teachers.

Math Resource Hub

Teacher-posted notes, JSS & Senior Secondary revision topics, practice prompts, and formula/skill checklists (Grade 6–10).

How to use: Choose your grade band, attempt the practice prompts, then review the key skills and common exam mistakes.

JSS Level (Grade 6–7): Number Sense, Algebra Starter & Geometry Skills

These foundations help learners build confidence with operations, patterns, measurements, and basic geometry.

1) Integers, Decimals & Fractions (Core)

  • Integers: ordering, addition/subtraction, multiplication/division, negative interpretation.
  • Fractions: simplifying, equivalent fractions, adding/subtracting with common denominators.
  • Decimals: place value, rounding, conversions between fractions and decimals.
  • Percentages: % as part-of-100; simple percentage problems.
  • Word problems: translate statements into operations.

Practice prompts:

  • Compute using correct order of operations (BODMAS).
  • Convert between fractions and decimals and then compare/ order them.
  • Solve percentage of a quantity: discounts, marks, and simple growth.

Suggested drill: Create a 10-minute daily set of fraction/decimal conversions and simplified results.

2) Ratio, Rate & Proportion (JSS Core Tools)

  • Ratio: simplifying ratios; sharing quantities in a ratio.
  • Rate: km/h, m/s; interpreting real-life rate meaning.
  • Proportion: direct proportion problems (as required progressively).

Practice prompts:

  • Split quantities (money, water, food) in ratios.
  • Convert units before calculating rate (avoid common mistakes).
  • Solve direct proportion problems with clear steps.

3) Algebra Starter: Expressions, Equations & Patterns

  • Algebraic expressions: like terms; simplifying.
  • Substitution: evaluate expressions for given values.
  • Equations (intro): solve simple linear equations (as required).
  • Patterns: number patterns; rules from patterns.

Practice prompts:

  • Substitute and simplify carefully (show working).
  • Solve one-step and two-step equations using inverse operations.
  • Find the next terms and the rule of patterns.

4) Geometry & Mensuration: Lines, Angles & 2D Shapes

  • Angles: properties of triangles; parallel line angle rules (core understanding).
  • Circles (intro): radius/diameter, simple circle properties.
  • Triangles: types by sides/angles; area of triangles (core).
  • Quadrilaterals: properties; area/perimeter basics.
  • Mensuration: rectangles/squares; area/perimeter practice.

Practice prompts:

  • Draw diagrams neatly and label angles clearly.
  • Solve area/perimeter problems with unit conversions where needed.

JSS Level (Grade 8–9): Algebra Expansion, Trigonometry Intro & Advanced Mensuration

Move from arithmetic into deeper algebraic manipulation and stronger geometry skills.

1) Algebra: Expanding, Factorizing (Core Skills)

  • Algebraic expansion: distributive property.
  • Factorization: take out common factors; simple identities where applicable.
  • Equations: solve linear equations and simultaneous equations (as required).
  • Indices (exponents): laws of indices (progressively introduced).

Practice prompts:

  • Expand and simplify expressions with correct sign handling.
  • Factorize to solve equations more quickly.
  • Check answers by substitution.

2) Number & Indices/Logs Foundations (As per Kenya progression)

  • Number systems: rational/irrational, surds (as introduced).
  • Indices/Surds simplification: turning problems into standard forms.
  • Standard forms: prepare for exam-style transformations.

Practice prompts:

  • Simplify surds and indices using rules.
  • Express answers in simplest form.

3) Geometry: 2D & 3D Shapes + Advanced Mensuration

  • 3D shapes: prisms, cylinders, cones, pyramids (surface area/volume where introduced).
  • Circles: circumference, area of circle (as required).
  • Coordinate geometry (intro): plotting points, distance between points (as required).

Practice prompts:

  • Solve volume/surface area problems and show formula substitution.
  • Use correct units (cm³ vs cm²).

4) Statistics & Probability (Core Exam Area)

  • Data handling: mean, median, mode (as required).
  • Graphs: bar graphs, line graphs, pie charts.
  • Probability: sample space; theoretical probability for equally likely outcomes.

Practice prompts:

  • Draw graphs from a given table.
  • Interpret data trends and answer question statements.
  • Compute probabilities and simplify fractions/decimals.

5) Trigonometry Intro (Where applicable)

  • Right triangles: opposite/adjacent/hypotenuse identification.
  • Angles: sine/cosine/tangent definitions (as introduced).
  • Basic trigonometric calculations: find missing sides/angles using ratios.

Practice prompts:

  • Identify sides correctly before substituting values.
  • Use calculator angles carefully (degrees vs radians).

Senior Secondary Entry (Grade 10 / Form 4): Exam-Ready Math Skills

A high-yield revision checklist aligned to common Grade 10 expectations: algebra, geometry, trigonometry, statistics and word problems.

1) Algebra: Functions, Transformations (as required) & Equations

  • Indices/Surds: simplification and standard form.
  • Quadratic foundations: solving and interpreting solutions (where introduced).
  • Functions: domain/range, interpreting graphs (as required).
  • Simultaneous equations: elimination/substitution methods.

Practice prompts:

  • Solve exam-style equations with clear steps.
  • Check solutions and interpret them in the question context.

2) Geometry & Trigonometry (High-Yield)

  • Coordinate geometry: straight line equations, gradient, intercepts (as required).
  • Trigonometry: solving right triangles and basic trig equations (as introduced).
  • Mensuration: composite shapes with volume/surface area methods.

Practice prompts:

  • Draw accurate diagrams and label given/required values.
  • Use correct trig ratio and sign conventions.
  • Solve composite shape problems with unit consistency.

3) Statistics, Probability & Data Interpretation

  • Measures of central tendency: mean/median/mode (as required).
  • Dispersion: range/interquartile basics (as required).
  • Probability: compound events (as required).
  • Interpreting questions: convert statements to calculations.

Practice prompts:

  • Compute probability for real scenario questions.
  • Summarize data in words and support with calculations.

4) Exam Strategy: Working, Neatness & Common Mistakes

  • Write known values and required values before solving.
  • Show working for algebraic simplification and substitutions.
  • Use units consistently for mensuration and measurement tasks.
  • Double-check calculator results, especially trig and surds.

Suggested drill: Do a timed mixed paper set twice weekly and correct every error.
